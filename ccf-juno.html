Terms & Concepts

Common Caused Faults are a special kind of 'fault', where more than one system is affected due to a common environmental or systemic characteristic. This kind of faults might appear in big interconnected systems as well. Causes might be zonal, regional, technological or even configuration-related. They might manifest commonalities from far away instants of time (chronologically speaking). An exagerated example: two SSD drives from the same manufacturer, technology and similar date of commissioning (e.g.: genesis of a blockchain) might start to present IO failures in the future around the same weeks or months. Summer and winter periods might also damage common components, etc.

This is a detector of concurrent missed blocks. It shall be never interpreted 1:1 with common caused faults, as there might well exist a perfect independence of events. Therefore, take this data with a grain of salt and (we hope) this might be useful in order to assess our network.
